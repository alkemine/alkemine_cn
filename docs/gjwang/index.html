    Machine Learning Potentials
    Machine learning potentials

    Classical large-scale molecular dynamics (CLMD) is an optimal choice to simulate the performance of real system, yet, in practices the lack of reliable and accurate interatomic potentials is Achilles heel. In this respect, it is an effective way to develop a method for fitting powerful potentials for CLMD by machine learning combined with big data and high-throughput calculations, which hence becomes a hot topic nowadays. PotentialMind (PM) is a general machine learning neural network potential framework for CLMD with DFT accuracy and is currently integrated into ALKEMIE.

    September 2016 - Present

    Phase-change Materials
    Phase-change materials

    Ge-Sb-Te (GST) alloys along the Sb2Te3-GeTe pseudo-binary compositional tie-line are phase-change materials (PCM), and are the most promising candidates for phase-change random-access memory (PCRAM), which is a kind of non-volatile rewritable data-storage memory based on rapid and reversible phase transitions between crystalline and amorphous phases, taking advantage of the difference in resistance properties between the crystalline (low-resistance, SET) and amorphous (high-resistance, RESET) states.

    September 2016 - Present

    Amorphous Structures and properties
    Amorphous structures and properties

    Amorphous solids have two characteristic properties. When cleaved or broken, they produce fragments with irregular, often curved surfaces; and they have poorly defined patterns when exposed to x-rays because their components are not arranged in a regular array. An amorphous, translucent solid is called a glass.
